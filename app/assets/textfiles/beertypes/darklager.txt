Dark lagers typically range in colour from amber to dark reddish brown, and may be termed Vienna, amber lager, dunkel, tmavé, or schwarzbier depending on region, colour or brewing method. Three of the most common dark lager types are of German origin: Dunkel which is characterized by a smooth malty flavour), Schwarzbier which has hints of chocolate or coffee flavours), and Bock which has a number of substyles.
